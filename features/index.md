# Features Overview

Printerhive started as a way to control and monitor multiple printers. Today it covers much more of the production workflow: files, queues, filament, orders, products, projects, integrations, and cost calculation.

## Core areas

### Printers and monitoring

Use Printerhive to see printer status, temperatures, progress, camera streams, AMS information, and current jobs from one dashboard. Bambu Lab, Klipper, and Prusa support varies by printer type. Start with [Supported Printers](/getting-started/supported-printers) and [Monitoring](/features/monitoring).

### Print queues and jobs

Printerhive separates current prints, queued work, and history. You can move jobs between queues, organize work by priority, and use printer matching to send jobs to suitable printers. See [Print queues and jobs](/printing/print-queues-and-jobs).

### Files and plates

Upload files once, organize them into folders and tags, inspect plates, and reuse them for products, projects, and orders. Printerhive supports direct flows from OrcaSlicer. See [Files, plates, and OrcaSlicer](/files/files-plates-orcaslicer).

### Filament and AMS

Track spools, stock, AMS trays, QR labels, low-filament situations, and filament mismatches before starting a print. See [Filament storage and AMS](/filament/filament-storage-and-ams).

### Orders, products, and projects

Use products for repeatable items and projects for customer or one-off work. Imported shop orders can be converted into printable order items and moved into queues. See [Orders, products, and projects](/production/orders-products-projects).

### E-commerce integrations

Printerhive can connect shop workflows such as Shopify, WooCommerce, Upgates, and CSV-based exports/imports. See [E-commerce integrations](/integrations/ecommerce-integrations).

### Print calculator

Create print profiles, configure costs, embed a calculator on your website, and collect quote inquiries. See [Print calculator](/calculator/print-calculator).
